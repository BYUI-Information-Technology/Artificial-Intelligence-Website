## Data Classification for AI Use

Understanding how to classify data is essential for responsible AI use. ( [See also the BYU-I Data Classification Policy](https://webmailbyui.sharepoint.com/sites/Policies/SitePages/Data%20Classification.aspx))

1. **Public Data:** Any data that can be found publicly on the web or can reasonably be accessed without credentials. CHOOSE WISELY; USE WITH ANY TOOL
2. **Internal or Confidential Data without PII:** Any data that you have access to and a reason for using, related to your role within the University. USE ONLY WITH BYU-IDAHO TOOLS
3. **Personally Identifiable Information (PII):** Any data that could potentially identify a specific individual, either on its own or when combined with other data. GET APPROVAL FROM THE AI GROUP
4. **Restricted:** Information of the highest sensitivity where inappropriate loss, changes, or disclosure could have grave consequences to the University or its students. DO NOT USE WITH AI

## STATEMENT OF POLICY

BYU-Idaho classifies data using four categories: **public, internal, confidential, and restricted**. This classification structure is in alignment with the other institutions within the Church Educational System (CES).

Data Stewards are responsible for classifying the data elements for which they have responsibility. If a data element classification becomes problematic to the University the data governance council can request a reclassification.

Data elements that have not been classified, by default will be classified as confidential.

## Quick Reference

1. PUBLIC - Information approved for public release that should be protected against loss or change
2. INTERNAL - Moderately sensitive information which is generally accessible within the University to those with a legitimate university purpose and is not intended for entities or persons outside the University. Note: This is the majority of all information that has been classified
3. CONFIDENTIAL - Highly sensitive information where an inappropriate loss, changes, or disclosure could have substantial consequences to the University
4. RESTRICTED - Information of the highest sensitivity where inappropriate loss, changes, or disclosure could have grave consequences to the University or its students

## DEFINITIONS

### PUBLIC

_Information approved for public release that should be protected against loss or change._

The following are only examples:

- Course catalog information
- University calendar dates
- Library bibliographic data (e.g. catalog)
- University department names and contact information
- Faculty curriculum vitae data:
  - Academic credentials
  - Published creative works and publication data
  - Published patent data
- Employees in leadership roles such as:
  - President
  - Vice Presidents
  - College Deans
  - Public Safety Director
- Basic campus maps, building abbreviations and addresses
- Standards based data such as:
  - International organization for Standardization (ISO) country codes
  - International Standard Book Number (ISBN)
  - Bank Routing Transit Numbers (RTN)
  - Merchant Category Codes (MCC)

### INTERNAL

_Moderately sensitive information which is generally accessible within the University to those with a legitimate university purpose and is not intended for entities or persons outside the University. Note: This is the majority of all information that has been classified._

The following are only examples:

- Student Records:
  - Academic record and Grades
  - Class (Freshman, etc.)
  - Scholarships/University Grants
  - Contact information and photos
- Organizational charts
- University Policies and procedures
- Employee identity and work information:
  - Active/Inactive, Part/Full time, Faculty/Staff/Admin, etc.
  - Employment actions (Hired, Terminated, Leave)
  - Birthday (date and month)
  - Contact information and photos
  - Time clock and hours worked data
  - Travel data
- Department budgets and financial transactions
- Credit Card last 4 digits
- Campus resources and scheduling

### CONFIDENTIAL

_Highly sensitive information where an inappropriate loss, changes, or disclosure could have substantial consequences to the University._

The following are only examples:

- Admissions questions and answers
- Ecclesiastical Endorsement

- Instructor and course ratings
- Performance and Continuing Faculty Status reviews
- Patent disclosure data
- Employee salary data
- Employee conflict of interest records
- Employment action reasons
- Age and Date of Birth (date, month and year)
- Ethnicity data about an individual
- Marital status of an individual
- Restricted people data
- Credit Cardholder Name, Address and Phone Number
- Church unit data for people (ward, stake, mission, leader, etc.)
- Detailed building floor plans
- Confidential communications
- Secret questions used to reset passwords

### RESTRICTED

_Information of the highest sensitivity where inappropriate loss, changes, or disclosure could have grave consequences to the University or its students._

The following are only examples:

- Social Security Numbers
- Credit Card number and/or associated verification number
- Bank account numbers
- Health Center medical records and clinical notes
- Data about people with disabilities or accommodations
- Personal Identification Numbers (PINs)
- Passwords and equivalents such as Private Keys and digital signatures
- The answers to secret questions used to reset passwords

## Impact Thresholds and Examples

### Substantial Consequences

Information disclosure that may be used to steal money from the University.

Disclosure of personal information that could lead to identity theft or monetary loss for a small set of individuals, representing a breach of professional expectations by the university.

Serious violation of regulatory requirements.

### Grave Consequences

Information disclosure that may be used to steal money from others who have entrusted the University with their information, including students, donors, employees, and other patrons.

Exposure of a significantly large amount of personal information that may result in significant fines or regulatory violations.

Disclosure of information that would put the school, employees, students, alumni, other patrons, or their families in physical danger.
