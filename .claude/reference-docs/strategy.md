# BYU-Idaho's AI Strategy

## Critical Requirements

- **Alignment with Prophetic Guidance**: Establish and monitor institution-wide principles and policies for AI use that align with Church guidance and BYU-Idaho’s mission. (see `.claude/reference-docs/church-guidance.md`)
- **Data Privacy and Security**: Ensure secure, scalable, and cost-effective technical foundations that protect institutional and student data.

## Priorities to Support Students

- **Advance Knowledge and Enhance Learning**: Integrate AI across teaching, learning, assessment, and curriculum in support of BYU-Idaho’s Institutional Learning Outcomes.
- **Develop Workforce Readiness**: Embed AI-enabled, industry-informed learning experiences into academic programs to prepare students for an AI-enabled workforce.
- **Enhance Student Support and Advising**: Use AI to enhance academic and career guidance, student support, and just-in-time access to information.

## Enabling Workstreams

- **AI Literacy and Fluency**: Build institutional literacy and fluency in AI through education, training, and leadership development.
- **R&D and Evaluation**: Continuously study the AI landscape, pilot and evaluate initiatives, assess progress on institutional priorities, incentivize innovation, and support evidence-informed decision-making.
- **Institutional Learning**: Intentionally share insights, practices, policies, and lessons learned across the university to accelerate learning, adoption, and cultural alignment.
- **IT Processes**: Develop IT processes that promote centralization of resources and support while enabling innovation.
